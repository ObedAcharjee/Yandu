Location

The Khowa-Bugun tribe, also known simply as the Bugun tribe, primarily inhabits the West Kameng district of Arunachal Pradesh in northeastern India. Their settlements are concentrated near Eaglenest Wildlife Sanctuary and Singchung Bugun Village Community Reserve (SBVCR) in the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas.

Geographic Location

  • The Bugun people live mainly in villages such as Singchung, Wanghoo, Muchut, and Namphri
  • Settlements are at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,000 meters above sea level
  • Their homeland sits along the Indo-Bhutan border region
  • The region is characterized by dense forests, valleys, and mountain slopes
  • Located in the Eastern Himalayan biodiversity hotspot
  • The territory covers approximately 17 square kilometers of community-reserved forest

Singchung Bugun Village Community Reserve

In 2017, the Bugun community established the Singchung Bugun Village Community Reserve (SBVCR), covering 17 square kilometers of their traditional land. This initiative won India's prestigious Biodiversity Award and demonstrates their commitment to preserving their natural heritage.

The reserve was created primarily to protect the critically endangered Bugun Liocichla bird, which was discovered in 2006 and is found nowhere else in the world. This bird species was named after the tribe, highlighting the intrinsic connection between the community and their environment.

How to Reach

  • Nearest Airport: Tezpur Airport (Assam), approximately 150 km
  • Nearest Railway Station: Tezpur Railway Station
  • Road Access: From Tezpur, take the road to Bomdila via Bhalukpong. The Bugun villages are located near Eaglenest Wildlife Sanctuary entrance.
  • Important Note: Visitors to Arunachal Pradesh require an Inner Line Permit (ILP) that can be obtained online or from designated government offices.
  • Best Time to Visit: October to April when the weather is pleasant and roads are generally accessible.
