Religion

Bomdila Monastery; Located in West Kameng District

The Buguns, originally animistic followers of the Donyi-Polo religion, adopted Mahayana Buddhism in the early 20th century under the influence of the Sherdukpen people, who introduced Tibetan Buddhist traditions. This shift saw Buddhist rituals integrated into communal practices, with lamas leading ceremonies. While many Buguns now identify as Buddhist in censuses, their faith remains a blend of Buddhist and animistic elements, reflecting a syncretic cultural evolution rooted in both old and new beliefs.

Though formally declaring Buddhism, the Buguns retain strong ties to their animistic heritage, including nature worship and ancestral rituals. Tibetan Buddhist influences from the Sherdukpens coexist with indigenous traditions, highlighting a dual identity where Buddhism masks enduring pre-Buddhist customs. This adaptive assimilation underscores their complex spiritual landscape rather than a complete abandonment of their origins.
